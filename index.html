I discover the beauty of music at an early age. I remember the first time I enjoyed singing was in primary year in school. Our teacher learned us a song for children. It is a canon, I adored the fact we could all sing in choir, and in canon.

When I was 14, it is the first time I learned to play my first three chords on a guitar. From that day, I still love to do it today. It gave me some calm, and a way to escape the harsh reality of my early years in life. It was not that easy, but with hindsight, I know it was all necessary.

And there is a saying, that without tending the strings and no pressure on it, we wouldn't able to hear the sound of it. With pain comes growth and beauty. And I discovered the mystery of recording when my mother offered me a tape recorder as a gift. I think I was between eight or ten years old.

That is when I heard my voice for the first time. It was a really weird experience. But with the time I would get used to it. Even enjoying it. It is the way I first learned to sing, because I could hear the wrong notes, and try it again, and again. It was a marvelous thing, and feeling.
